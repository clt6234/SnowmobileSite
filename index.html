Who Are We?

Our team is built up of 25 plus individuals of various majors from Mechanical Engineering to Computer Science to Graphic Design. Eric Oswald our team’s Lead Engine Designer is a 3rd year Mechanical Engineering Technology major who joined our team because he is passionate about snowmobiles, engines, and green technology. Jason Ganje a 2nd year Electro/Mechanical Engineering Technology major and our team’s Team Manager stated “I grew up riding four wheelers and I want my grand kids to experience powersports in the future.” The rest of our team has a strong passion for powersports and are interested in putting in their part to help conserve our environment and national parks. Our team was founded in the fall of 2012 by four fearless Engineering students, Mark Menzenski, John Bulzacchelli, Zack Kraenzle, and Tom Mordovancey. Polaris Industries donated a snowmobile chassis and a four stroke engine and we were tasked to make it work. Unfortunately, due to lack of funds and available space, the team was not able to get the snowmobile running properly for the 2013 and 2014 Clean Snowmobile Challenge. For the 2015 competition our team was able to compete and placed 12th. Our momentum for the 2016 competition was greater than ever and we were able to place 10th, as well as bringing back an emissions analyser from Horiba.

What Do We Do?

The main goal of the team is to make a snowmobile that is more environmentally friendly than the stock snowmobiles currently on the market. We begin with a stock snowmobile, and from there re-engineer everything from what engine we put into it all the way down to the size of the track that we want to have on it.

About The Competition

To show you how the Clean Snowmobile Challenge was started, we will go back to the mid 1990’s where Yellowstone National Park was a major attraction for snowmobilers, where about 90,000 people visited by snowmobile each winter. In the year 2000 the National Park Service banned snowmobiles in Yellowstone due to snowmobiles causing damage to the park’s wildlife, air quality, and natural soundscapes. In the year 2003 snowmobiles were allowed back into the park, but a limited number of snowmobiles could be let in, they had to be lead by a guide, and had to be of the Best Available Technology (BAT). The Clean Snowmobile Challenge was also created in 2003 to help create quiet and clean burning snowmobiles for pristine areas like Yellowstone.
